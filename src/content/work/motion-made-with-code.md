You look at a screen all day and barely notice the small movements on it — a number ticking up, a bar filling in, a little spinner going round. I started making those with code a couple of years ago, one at a time, and somewhere along the way one at a time turned into hundreds.

They ended up sorting themselves into four collections. Counters and charts in the first. Old computer screens rebuilt from memory in the second. Everyday apps faked so carefully they pass for screen recordings in the third — and then, to close, the same quiet motion slowed almost to stillness and pointed at old sacred patterns. They look like four different projects, and in some ways they are. But underneath they run on the same handful of habits, just aimed at very different things. That was the part I most wanted to find out: whether a way of working that suits a business chart could also draw a mandala, or bring back a 1995 desktop. It could. Here they all are, in one place — watch them in whatever order you like.
