I wanted a single object to carry the whole weight of this one — no cast, no dialogue, just one reflective sphere present at every point along an impossibly long timeline. Not as a narrator. As a witness that never once looks away.

The orb opens in the silence before anything exists, catching the first light of a universe still deciding what it's going to be. From there it drifts through the scale of deep time — ancient jungles and creatures too large to feel real, then further forward to a first fire and the first faces gathered around it, then further still into the noise and neon of everything we've built since. Through all of it the orb does the same thing it's always done: it reflects whatever's in front of it, and keeps moving.

What changes is what happens once it's carried that much history. On a quiet mountaintop, far from anything it's already seen, the orb stops being able to simply reflect the world back and becomes something that stands inside it instead — a figure of light where a mirror used to be. The idea I kept returning to while making this: you don't get to witness everything and remain unchanged by it forever. Eventually the watching becomes part of what's being watched.
