Whenever the cost of making something collapses, people predict the end of the people who made it. It rarely plays out that way, as far as I can tell. Value doesn't disappear when production gets cheap. It relocates, usually to whatever the cheap thing still can't do. Photography didn't end painting — it freed painting to stop being about accurate depiction at all. Cheap generation won't end design either. But it will move the value, and the only real question is whether you move with it.

## Value relocates; it doesn't evaporate

The mistake is assuming the value was ever in the making. Mostly it wasn't. The making was just the expensive bottleneck that made the surrounding judgment look free by comparison. Remove the bottleneck and suddenly the judgment is where all the visible value sits, because it's what's left that's still scarce.

<div class="table-wrap">

| Loses value as generation gets free | Gains value as generation gets free |
|---|---|
| Producing variations | Choosing the right one |
| Executing a defined brief | Defining the brief |
| Technical skill at making | Judgment about what's worth making |
| Speed of output | Quality of taste |
| "I can build it" | "I know what should be built, and whether it's any good" |

</div>

## The jobs don't vanish — they change shape

Concretely, I think fewer people get paid purely to produce, and more get paid to direct — to decide, curate, define, and judge. The title "designer" survives, but its center of gravity shifts from craft-as-production toward craft-as-judgment. That's disruptive if your whole value was production speed. It's an opportunity if you were always secretly more interested in the deciding than the pixel-pushing anyway.

This is the bigger version of a personal shift I've written about from a few angles — [why taste got expensive](/blog/ai-made-creation-cheap-taste-got-expensive/), and [what separates the designer AI amplifies from the one it replaces](/blog/the-designer-who-uses-ai-vs-the-one-ai-could-replace/). The through-line is the same at every scale I've looked at it. As making approaches free, the premium moves to knowing what to make and whether it's actually good. Point your career at the column that's gaining value, not the one that's losing it.
