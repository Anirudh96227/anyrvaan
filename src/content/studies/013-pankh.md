Most devotion gets rewarded eventually — some miracle, some proof that the waiting meant something. This one never offers that. It just asks whether staying with someone, noticing the small things about them, was always the actual point.

Three and a half minutes of a song for Krishna, circling his flute and his feather, with no climax written in anywhere. It holds your attention on nothing but presence, for longer than feels comfortable, and somehow that turns out to be enough.
