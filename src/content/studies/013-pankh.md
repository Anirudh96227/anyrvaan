## Core Question

Does "hold before the arrival" survive being stretched from nine seconds to three and a half minutes, or does something have to happen eventually?

## Concept

A full devotional song for Krishna, its lyrics circling his flute and his peacock feather — *pankh* — without ever cashing that build-up in on a depicted miracle or a narrated turning point. The song stays in praise and noticing: the flute, the feather, the color of dusk. No climax arrives, because none was ever the plan.

## Why This Is the Long One

Every other clip in the reel proves its point in a handful of seconds, which makes it easy to assume the trick only works because it's short. Pankh is the control test — three and a half minutes with nowhere to hide, and it holds anyway, because "devotion as sustained noticing" doesn't need a payoff to stay interesting.

## Connection to the Cuts

Closes the devotional trio started by The Bow That Hasn't Landed Yet and A Merge in Progress, and answers the question they raise: reverence rendered as attention rather than spectacle isn't a short-form gimmick. It just keeps going.
