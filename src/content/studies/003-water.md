## Core Question

Can water that's perfectly still still be about to move?

## Concept

A locked shot over glass-flat water — a pond, a puddle, a held breath of a surface.

A single ripple crosses it from somewhere off-frame, the way Study 008's pulse later moves through an entire landscape at once. For the moment it passes, the light refracting through it distorts into something legible. Then the ring fades, the surface settles, and the water is mirror-still again — unreadable, the way it started.

## The Hidden Word

**Begin.**

Water never holds a shape on its own; it only ever shows you what's disturbing it. The word appearing here isn't drawn onto the surface — it's the surface's honest report of something happening nearby. Third in the sequence, at the rough midpoint of the search, it's less an answer than a nudge: noticing is itself a beginning, not a conclusion.

## Visual Principles

- Locked camera, eye-level or directly overhead.
- One ripple, one source, never explained or shown.
- The word exists only in refracted light — nothing is drawn on the water itself.
- The surface returns to total stillness by the final frame.

## Connection to Anyrvaan

Water is the site's oldest metaphor — the mist that opens the studio's language, the stillness the homepage asks you to enter before anything moves. This is that idea, unhidden: the surface was never doing nothing. It was only waiting for a reason to show it.
