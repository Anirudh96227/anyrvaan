## Core Question

How little of a character can you show and still have someone swear they met a person?

## Concept

Six seconds. A pure silhouette, wrapped in shifting purple and pink flame that won't sit still long enough to be described. No face, no defining feature, no hint of backstory — just a shape insisting on itself through fire that keeps changing its mind.

## Why It's Nobody on Purpose

The instant you name this thing — hero, villain, spirit — the tension the whole reel depends on collapses. Left as unlit silhouette and unstable color, the figure stays exactly where every other clip here also lives: present, forceful, and refusing to be cast in anything yet.

## Connection to the Cuts

The shortest, most stripped-down entry in the set, and maybe the purest proof of the idea — with no mythology to lean on and no plot to hint at, Nobody, on Fire has nothing to make its point with except the becoming itself.
