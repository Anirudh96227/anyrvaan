## Core Question

Who is writing, when the wind is the only hand?

## Concept

A locked wide shot across a dune, fine sand stretching to the edge of frame. The wind that moves it is never shown — only its effect.

A thin skin of surface grains lifts and resettles, and for a moment the motion traces a word into the dune's existing ripple pattern, before the same wind erases it back into ordinary texture.

## The Hidden Word

**Shift.**

Sand is the most literally restless material in the series — it looks fixed from a distance and is never actually still up close, grain by grain, even at rest. That's the whole idea: the word doesn't interrupt the dune's stillness, it's made of the same motion the dune never stopped having.

## Visual Principles

- Locked wide framing; no visible wind source, ever.
- The word must read as an accident of erosion, never as calligraphy.
- Grain-level real motion only — no compositing, no overlay.
- The dune's texture after the reveal is indistinguishable from before it.

## Connection to Anyrvaan

This site's own type system doesn't hold fixed sizes at fixed breakpoints — it's built on fluid, continuously adjusting scales that shift with the viewport instead of snapping between fixed states. Sand is that same idea, outdoors: nothing here is really fixed, no matter how solid the dune looks from a distance.
