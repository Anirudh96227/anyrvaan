Enlightenment is supposed to look a certain way — the glow, the stillness, the moment everything resolves. What's rarely shown is the instant right before it, when someone is just sitting there, waiting for whatever's about to happen to them.

Six seconds. A lotus lit from somewhere inside itself, still opening. No glow-up, no final frame, no caption telling you it worked. Just enough time to notice something is happening, before anyone tells you what it means.
