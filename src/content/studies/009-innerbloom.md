## Core Question

If you had to film the exact middle of enlightenment — not the sitting down, not the glow-up — what would even be in frame?

## Concept

Six seconds. A sage, eyes closed, beneath a lotus that's mid-bloom and lit from somewhere inside itself. No before shot of the bud closed. No after shot of it fully open. Just the part every other version of this image cuts around: light still gathering, form still deciding what it is.

## The Punchline That Never Lands

Every stock image of a meditating sage exists to deliver one punchline — *and then he became enlightened.* This clip is the setup with the punchline surgically removed. Six seconds is barely enough time to read the image, let alone let it resolve into a caption. That's the whole trick: too short to become a poster.

## Connection to the Cuts

This is the opening splice, and it sets the rule the rest of the reel follows without exception — nothing here gets to finish. Innerbloom is the gentlest version of that rule: no camera move, no reveal, just a moment left running exactly as long as it takes to notice something's still in motion.
