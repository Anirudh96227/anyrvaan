## Core Question

Is a bow more devotional while it's still happening, or once it's finished and can be photographed?

## Concept

Nine seconds. Hanuman kneels before Shiva as Nataraja, a stotram playing underneath, the gesture held at the kneel rather than let all the way down into a full prostration. Respect caught mid-descent — still falling, not yet landed.

## Why the Frame Won't Finish the Gesture

A completed bow is a photograph — an event already over by the time you see it. A bow still descending is a verb — something the viewer arrives inside of, not after. Every devotional image you've ever seen shows the former. This one insists on the latter.

## Connection to the Cuts

First of three devotional pieces in the reel (with A Merge in Progress and the longer Pankh), and it sets their shared bit: divinity delivered through a held posture instead of a finished spectacle. Nothing transforms here — the "becoming" is reverence itself, caught taking physical shape one frame before it's done.
